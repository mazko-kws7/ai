An RSI below 30 is considered oversold, which might signal a potential buying opportunity. The RSI is calculated using the following formulas: 1. Calculate price changes: * First, determine the upward change (U) and downward change (D) for each period. * If the current close is higher than the previous close: U = current close - previous close, D = 0. * If the current close is lower than the previous close: D = previous close - current close, U = 0. * If the current close is the same as the previous close: U = 0, D = 0. 2. Calculate average upward and downward changes: * Calculate the average upward change (AvgU) and average downward change (AvgD) over the past N periods (typically 14 periods). This is usually done using an exponential moving average to give more weight to recent data. 3. Calculate Relative Strength (RS): * RS = AvgU / AvgD 4. Calculate RSI: * RSI = 100 - (100 / (1 + RS))

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence): MACD is a trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages of a price. The MACD is calculated by subtracting the 26-period exponential moving average (EMA) from the 12-period EMA.

Bollinger Bands: Bollinger Bands consist of: 1. A middle band being a 20-period simple moving average (SMA). 2. An upper band at two standard deviations above the middle band. 3. A lower band at two standard deviations below the middle band.

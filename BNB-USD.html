  • RSI (Relative Strength Index):
    • Values of 70 or above suggest an overbought condition, potentially indicating a trend reversal or correction.
    • Values of 30 or below suggest an oversold condition, potentially indicating an undervalued asset.
  • MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence):
    • A bullish signal occurs when the MACD line crosses above its signal line; a bearish signal occurs when the MACD line crosses below its signal line.
    • Potential buy signals occur when the MACD moves above zero, and potential sell signals when it crosses below zero.
  • Moving Averages (MA):
    • A bullish crossover (50-day MA moving above the 200-day MA) is called a golden cross and signals a new bullish trend.
    • A bearish crossover (50-day MA crosses below the 200-day MA) is known as the death cross and indicates a bear market may be starting.

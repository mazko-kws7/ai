I have researched the formulas for the technical indicators. Heres a summary:

  • Simple Moving Average (SMA): The average price over a specified period. Its calculated by adding the closing prices for the period and dividing by the number of periods.
    • Formula: SMA = (Sum of closing prices over N periods) / N
  • Relative Strength Index (RSI): A momentum indicator that measures the magnitude of recent price changes to evaluate overbought or oversold conditions. It oscillates between 0 and 100.
    • Formula: RSI = 100 - [100 / (1 + RS)]
      • Where: RS = Average gain of up periods / Average loss of down periods
  • Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD): A trend-following momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages of prices.
    • MACD Line: 12-period EMA - 26-period EMA
    • Signal Line: 9-period EMA of the MACD line
    • Histogram: MACD Line - Signal Line

1. Daily Chart Analysis (Last Three Months)

  • Moving Averages (MA): Id calculate the 50-day and 200-day moving averages. These are lagging indicators that help identify the overall trend. A shorter MA crossing above a longer MA is generally a bullish signal (golden cross), while the opposite is bearish (death cross).
  • Relative Strength Index (RSI): RSI measures the magnitude of recent price changes to evaluate overbought or oversold conditions. An RSI above 70 is considered overbought, and below 30 is considered oversold.
    • Formula: RSI = 100 - [100 / (1 + RS)] where RS = Average gain of up periods / Average loss of down periods
  • MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence): MACD identifies potential buy and sell signals based on the convergence and divergence of two moving averages (typically 12-day and 26-day EMA).
    • Formula: MACD Line = 12-day EMA - 26-day EMA; Signal Line = 9-day EMA of MACD Line
  • Trend Lines: Draw trend lines connecting higher lows in an uptrend or lower highs in a downtrend. Breaks of these trend lines can signal a change in trend.

  2. Relative Strength Index (RSI):
    • RSI measures the magnitude of recent price changes to evaluate overbought or oversold conditions in the price of an asset.
    • An RSI above 70 indicates overbought conditions, suggesting a potential sell signal.
    • An RSI below 30 indicates oversold conditions, suggesting a potential buy signal.
  3. Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD):
    • MACD is calculated by subtracting the 26-period Exponential Moving Average (EMA) from the 12-period EMA. A 9-period EMA of the MACD (the signal line) is then plotted on top of the MACD.
    • Bullish: MACD line crosses above the signal line.
    • Bearish: MACD line crosses below the signal line.
  4. Bollinger Bands:
    • A volatility indicator consisting of a simple moving average (SMA) and two bands plotted at a standard deviation above and below the SMA.
    • If the price touches or exceeds the upper band, the asset may be overbought.
    • If the price touches or exceeds the lower band, the asset may be oversold.

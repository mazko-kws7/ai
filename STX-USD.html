  • Simple Moving Averages (SMA):
    • 50-day SMA: This smooths out the price data over the last 50 days, giving an idea of the medium-term trend.
    • 200-day SMA: This provides a longer-term trend perspective.
    • Interpretation: If the 50-day SMA is above the 200-day SMA, it suggests an upward trend. If the 50-day SMA is below the 200-day SMA, it suggests a downward trend.
  • Relative Strength Index (RSI):
    • Calculation: RSI = 100 - [100 / (1 + RS)] where RS = Average gain of up periods / Average loss of down periods over the past 14 days.
    • Interpretation:
      • RSI > 70: Overbought condition, suggesting a potential pullback or reversal.
      • RSI < 30: Oversold condition, suggesting a potential bounce or reversal.
      • RSI between 30 and 70: Neutral condition, indicating the trend may continue.
  • Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD):
    • Calculation:
      • MACD Line: 12-day EMA - 26-day EMA
      • Signal Line: 9-day EMA of MACD Line
      • MACD Histogram: MACD Line - Signal Line
    • Interpretation:
      • MACD Line crosses above Signal Line: Bullish signal (potential buy).
      • MACD Line crosses below Signal Line: Bearish signal (potential sell).
      • MACD Histogram increasing: Bullish momentum.
      • MACD Histogram decreasing: Bearish momentum.
  • Bollinger Bands:
    • Calculation:
      • Middle Band: 20-day SMA
      • Upper Band: 20-day SMA + (2 * Standard Deviation)
      • Lower Band: 20-day SMA - (2 * Standard Deviation)
    • Interpretation:
      • Price near the Upper Band: Overbought condition, potential sell signal.
      • Price near the Lower Band: Oversold condition, potential buy signal.
      • Band squeeze (bands getting closer): Indicates low volatility; a breakout is likely.
      • Band widening (bands moving apart): Indicates high volatility; trend is strengthening.
  • Fibonacci Retracement Levels:
    • Identification: Identify a significant swing high and swing low on the chart.
    • Common Levels: 23.6%, 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%, 78.6%
    • Interpretation:
      • These levels act as potential support and resistance.
      • Price often retraces to these levels before continuing the trend.

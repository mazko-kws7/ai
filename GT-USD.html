2. Technical Analysis:

  • Moving Averages (MA): Moving averages smooth out price data and can help identify trends. A rising MA indicates an uptrend, while a falling MA indicates a downtrend. Crossovers between short-term and long-term MAs can also provide signals. For example, a Golden Cross (50-day MA crossing above 200-day MA) suggests a bullish trend.
  • Relative Strength Index (RSI): The RSI measures the magnitude of recent price changes to evaluate overbought or oversold conditions in the market. An RSI above 70 suggests overbought conditions (potential sell signal), and an RSI below 30 suggests oversold conditions (potential buy signal).
  • Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD): The MACD is a momentum indicator that shows the relationship between two moving averages of prices. It can help identify trend changes, and potential entry and exit points. A bullish crossover (MACD line crossing above the signal line) indicates a potential buy signal, while a bearish crossover (MACD line crossing below the signal line) indicates a potential sell signal.
  • Bollinger Bands (BB): Bollinger Bands are volatility indicators that consist of a moving average and two bands that are standard deviations away from the moving average. When the price reaches or surpasses the upper band, it may be overbought, suggesting a potential selling opportunity. When the price falls below the lower Bollinger Band, a buy signal may be given.
  • Fibonacci Retracements: Fibonacci retracement levels are used to identify potential support and resistance levels. Key Fibonacci ratios include 23.6%, 38.2%, 50%, 61.8%, and 78.6%. These levels can act as psychological barriers for traders.

Technical Analysis

Technical indicators help traders make sense of price data by analyzing how the price of an asset is behaving. These indicators can be grouped into volume, momentum, trend, and volatility. Common indicators include Moving Averages, RSI, MACD, Bollinger Bands, and Fibonacci retracements.

  • Moving Averages (MA): Used to identify the direction of a trend. Rising MAs suggest uptrends, while falling MAs indicate downtrends.
  • Relative Strength Index (RSI): A momentum indicator used to identify overbought or oversold conditions. Readings above 70 often indicate overbought conditions (potential sell signal), while readings below 30 suggest oversold conditions (potential buy signal).
  • Moving Average Convergence Divergence (MACD): Combines trend and momentum analysis, useful for identifying potential buy and sell signals.
  • Bollinger Bands: Help identify potential overbought and oversold conditions and measure market volatility.
  • Fibonacci Retracement: Used to identify potential support and resistance levels.

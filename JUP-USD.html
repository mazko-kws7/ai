Technical Analysis

Technical indicators are mathematical calculations based on price, volume, or open interest and are used to forecast future price movements.

Common indicators include moving averages for trend analysis, the Relative Strength Index (RSI) for identifying overbought or oversold conditions, and On Balance Volume (OBV) for assessing directional volume.

Trend: * Moving Averages (MAs) are key trend indicators, smoothing price data to show clear trend directions. * Rising MAs suggest uptrends, and falling MAs indicate downtrends.

Momentum:

  • Momentum indicators measure the speed of price movements and help identify overbought or oversold conditions, often signaling potential reversals.
  • The Stochastic Oscillator measures price momentum over a specific period. Readings above 80 indicate an overbought market, and below 20 suggest oversold conditions.
  • The Relative Strength Index (RSI) is a simple line graph that oscillates between 0 to 100, with 50 being neutral. Overbought conditions are indicated using higher values, while lower values are indicators of oversold conditions.

Volatility:

  • Bollinger bands are used to gauge market volatility. When market volatility increases, the bands expand, and during less volatile times, they narrow.

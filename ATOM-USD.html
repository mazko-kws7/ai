Technical Analysis

Technical indicators are tools that help traders make sense of price data, analyzing how the price of an asset is behaving. Common indicators include moving averages for trend analysis, the Relative Strength Index (RSI) for identifying overbought or oversold conditions, and On Balance Volume (OBV) for assessing directional volume.

  • Moving Averages: Indicate upward, downward, or sideways trends.
  • Bollinger Bands: Used to gauge market volatility. The bands expand when market volatility increases and narrow during less volatile times. Breaking the lower band is interpreted as a buy signal.
  • MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence): Combines trend and momentum analysis.
  • RSI (Relative Strength Index): Measures the speed and change of price movements. An RSI below 30 indicates an oversold condition, while an RSI above 70 suggests overbought conditions. The 50 level often acts as support and resistance for momentum.
